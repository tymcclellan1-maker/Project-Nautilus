Ichthyology: The Study of Fish

Osteichthyes (Bony Fish)

Osteichthyes, or bony fish, are the largest class of vertebrates, with over 28,000 species. They have skeletons primarily made of bone, and most species have scales and swim bladders for buoyancy. Examples include salmon, trout, and goldfish.

Agnathans (Jawless Fish)

Agnathans are primitive fish that lack jaws. They include species such as lampreys and hagfish. These fish have elongated, eel-like bodies and are known for their unique feeding mechanisms, such as sucking or scavenging.

Chondrichthyes (Cartilaginous Fish)

Chondrichthyes are fish with skeletons made of cartilage instead of bone. This group includes sharks, rays, and skates. They are known for their streamlined bodies, sharp teeth, and keen senses, making them efficient predators.

Additional Information

Ichthyology is the branch of zoology that focuses on the study of fish. It encompasses the biology, behavior, and ecology of fish species. Researchers in this field play a critical role in understanding aquatic ecosystems and conserving marine biodiversity.
