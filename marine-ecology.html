Marine Ecology

About Marine Ecology

Marine ecology is the study of the interactions between marine organisms and their environment. It encompasses ecosystems such as coral reefs, mangroves, and the deep sea, and explores the relationships between species and their habitats.

Marine Life

Jobs in Marine Ecology

  • Marine Biologist: Research marine organisms and their ecosystems. Salary: $50,000 - $90,000/year
  • Oceanographer: Study the physical and chemical properties of the ocean. Salary: $60,000 - $100,000/year
  • Environmental Consultant: Advise on marine conservation projects. Salary: $45,000 - $85,000/year

Average Salaries

The average salary for professionals in marine ecology varies depending on the role and experience. Entry-level positions start at around $45,000/year, while senior roles can exceed $100,000/year.
