Marine Mammology

What is Marine Mammology?

Marine mammology is the study of marine mammals, including whales, dolphins, seals, and manatees. It focuses on their behavior, physiology, ecology, and conservation. Marine mammologists play a critical role in understanding and protecting these fascinating creatures and their habitats.

Job Opportunities in Marine Mammology

Marine mammologists can work in various fields, including research, conservation, education, and rehabilitation. They may work for universities, government agencies, non-profit organizations, or aquariums.

Average Salaries

The average salary for marine mammologists varies depending on experience and location. Entry-level positions typically start at around $40,000 per year, while experienced professionals can earn upwards of $90,000 per year.
